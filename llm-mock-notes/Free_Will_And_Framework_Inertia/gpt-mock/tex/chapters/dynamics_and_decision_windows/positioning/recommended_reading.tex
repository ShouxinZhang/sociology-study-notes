\section{Recommended Reading}

\begin{readingbox}
\textbf{Foundation} Use these to understand why barrier geometry, instability,
and transition timing matter more than slogans about intensity alone.
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item Steven Strogatz, \emph{Nonlinear Dynamics and Chaos}.
  \item Eugene Izhikevich, \emph{Dynamical Systems in Neuroscience}.
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Modern Research} Read these after the chapter logic is clear; they help
the reader connect everyday decision windows to metastability and biological
transition dynamics.
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item Nature Neuroscience work on predictable bifurcation dynamics during
        falling asleep.
  \item Review work on metastability in brain dynamics.
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Reading discipline} This chapter contributes the timing logic. Use
Chapter~\ref{ch:planning} to study how windows are prepared before they open,
and Chapter~\ref{ch:consciousness-biology} to see why state shifts can be read
as serious transition problems rather than motivational rhetoric.
\end{readingbox}
