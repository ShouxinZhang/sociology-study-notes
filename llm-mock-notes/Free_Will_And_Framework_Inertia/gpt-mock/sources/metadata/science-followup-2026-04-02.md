# Science And Science Advances Follow-Up

## Search Goal

Strengthen venue coverage for the research-backed manuscript by checking whether
`Science` or `Science Advances` provides strong-fit sources for:

1. consciousness-state transitions,
2. emergence relevant to framework formation,
3. advanced brain or neural dynamics relevant to the mathematical layer.

## Discovery Method

1. attempted direct `science.org` search and DOI-PDF retrieval,
2. confirmed that `science.org` search endpoints return access challenges in
   this environment,
3. used OpenAlex as a metadata-discovery fallback, then mapped results back to
   official DOI or journal pages.

## Result

### High-Fit Outcome

No high-fit `Science` or `Science Advances` article was identified that clearly
outperforms the current Nature-family and arXiv sources for the manuscript's
main consciousness / emergence / dynamical-systems spine.

### Adjacent Candidate

- `SCIADV-2024-neural-dynamics-predictive-timing`
  - DOI: `10.1126/sciadv.adi2525`
  - Title: `Neural dynamics of predictive timing and motor engagement in music listening`
  - Assessment: adjacent neuroscience / dynamics venue hit, but not a strong
    direct fit for the core consciousness-state chapter
  - Retrieval status: official PDF request returned `403` in this environment

### Low-Value Or Off-Target Hits

OpenAlex venue-filtered queries for `Science Advances` returned many weak or
off-topic matches, indicating that venue-follow-up alone is not a reliable
quality filter for this project.

## Decision

1. Keep `Science / Science Advances` as a monitored venue, not as a mandatory
   source supplier.
2. Do not dilute the core source map with low-fit venue hits.
3. Continue to prioritize stronger-fit Nature-family empirical papers and the
   already downloaded arXiv mathematical extensions.

## Business Outcome

This follow-up closes an open planning loop without damaging source quality.
The manuscript can now say that `Science` venue coverage was actively checked,
but the core evidence chain remains driven by fit and explanatory value rather
than prestige-by-journal-name.
