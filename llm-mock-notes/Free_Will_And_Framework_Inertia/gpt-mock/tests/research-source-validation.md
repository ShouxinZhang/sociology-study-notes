## Fast Failure Rules

- Fail if an English claim cannot be traced to both the literature map and the
  source index.
- Fail if Chapter 2 presents heuristic formulas or barrier scaffolds as if they
  were stronger than the current evidence allows.
- Fail if Chapter 1 blurs descriptive notation, heuristic scaffold, monotone
  assumptions, and unsupported-if-overstated claims into one undifferentiated
  formal voice.
- Fail if Chapter 3 contains theorem-style claims with no assumptions, no proof
  support, or stale dependency on an obsolete formal interface.
- Fail if textbook or modelling-language support is treated as direct empirical
  validation for Chapter 1 or Chapter 3.
- Fail if a case or intervention rule is presented as strong empirical proof
  when it is only design inference.
- Fail if Chapter 5 or Chapter 6 uses deeper theory / math vocabulary as if
  that alone upgrades behavioural claims into strong proof.
- Fail if the Chinese review upgrades speculative material into core evidence.
- Fail if frontier material leaks from the appendix into the empirical lane.
- Fail if a chapter loses its reading block or its reading block no longer
  matches the chapter's teaching job.

## Sign-Off Standard

Pass only if the research chapters are traceable, the formal-model chapters are
honest about heuristic and theorem boundaries, the application chapters are
source-aware without overstating proof strength, the Chinese review preserves
meaning and evidence weight, frontier material stays fenced off, and
recommended reading still aligns with the chapter architecture.
