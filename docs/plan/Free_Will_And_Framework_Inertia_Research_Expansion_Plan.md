## Why This Plan Exists

The current `gpt-mock/tex/` manuscript already improved the table of contents,
cases, and pedagogy. That solved the first business problem: the text now looks
more like a textbook prototype than a short essay. The next business problem is
different. The manuscript still lacks a serious research backbone in three
areas the user explicitly requested:

1. consciousness frontier biology and neuroscience,
2. emergence as a mechanism for framework formation,
3. advanced dynamical systems as the mathematical upgrade path.

This new plan exists to expand the book without collapsing its credibility. The
manuscript should not mix strong empirical neuroscience, theoretical systems
language, and speculative consciousness-physics proposals as if they were equal.
The new planning cycle therefore uses evidence grading and chapter-level source
placement.

## Core Planning Policy

The new manuscript should organize sources into four lanes:

1. `strong empirical core`
   Biological and neuroscience evidence suitable for the main manuscript.
2. `mechanism-building theory`
   Emergence and dynamical-systems sources that strengthen explanation.
3. `speculative frontier`
   High-risk physics or architecture theories that must be clearly isolated.
4. `classic textbook / review layer`
   Per-chapter recommended reading appendices that improve educational depth.

## Recommended Reading Policy

Each major chapter should end with a short appendix-style reading block:

1. `Foundation`
   One classic or textbook-style source.
2. `Modern Research`
   One or two high-value papers or reviews.
3. `Frontier`
   Optional, clearly marked, only when the chapter benefits from a speculative extension.

This structure makes the manuscript more valuable as a textbook product: it
teaches the reader where the ideas came from and where to go next.

## Test Requirements

The new cycle must add tests beyond the previous plan:

1. `Evidence separation test`
   Empirical, theoretical, and speculative sources must never be merged without labels.
2. `Chapter destination test`
   Every source in the literature map must have a chapter destination.
3. `Reading appendix test`
   Every major chapter must have at least one classic reading and one modern reading when appropriate.
4. `Math justification test`
   Every advanced dynamical-systems tool must have an explanatory role in the manuscript.
5. `Integration test`
   New research chapters must reinforce the book's main framework instead of replacing it with disconnected surveys.
